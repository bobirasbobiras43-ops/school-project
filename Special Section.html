Sakura cherry tree

Special Section

Sakura桜 or 櫻 refers to flowering cherry tree of the genus Prunus, especially Prunus serrulata, celebrated for their pale pink to white spring blooms which are considered a national symbol of Japan, representing the transient beauty of life and the renewal of nature each spring. The act of viewing them is called Hanami (flower viewing) and is the most famous thing to do in Japan especially between March and April. Tourists can also view many sites that specialize in predicting Hanami in different areas in Japan making tourists plan their whole trip around seeing the phenomenon. Maruyama Park is one of the most famous places for its vibrant hanami gathering each spring and its large weeping cherry tree and night illuminations.
