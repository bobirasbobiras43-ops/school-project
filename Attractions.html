Top Japan Attractions 🇯🇵

🟢 1. Mount Fuji(富士山/富士の山)

Mount fuji is the tallest mountain in Japan, standing at 3,776 above sea level and is considered a symbol of Japan. It last erupted in 1707 yet it still active to this day and stands out because it is made up of three different volcanoes. The mountain is surrounded by 5 lakes, Kawaguchiko(河口湖), the most popular and accesible one, Saiko(西湖), meaning the west lake, lake Yamanakako(山中湖) the largest, Shojiko (精進湖), the smallest and Motosuko (本栖湖) the deepest. There are 4 trails that lead to the top of the mountain, making climbing a popular activity amongs hikers and visitors. Visitors can also explore nearby attractions such as Oshino Hakkai, a small vilage in a scenic setting with 8 ponds and features views of Mount Fuji for amazing photos. There is also Arakurayama Sengen Park, which offers dramatic views of Mt. Fuji along with nearby seasonal cherry blossoms and a pagoda(a traditional Japanese tower).

🟤 2. Fushimi Inari Taisha(伏見稲荷大社)

Fushimi Inari Taisha(伏見稲荷大社) is one of the most famous shinto shrines in Japan, located Fushimi-ku, Kyoto. It was founded in 711 CE by the Hata clan and is considered the head shrine of 30,000 inari shrines. The shrine is dedicated to Inari Ōkami,the deity of rice, prosperity and business success. It is best known for its thousands of vermilion(bright red) torii gated forming scening tunnels along the wooded slopes of Mount Inari, offering an unforgetable walking experience for visitors.

🔵 3. Kinkaku-Ji(金閣寺)

Kinkaku-Ji(金閣寺), also known as the Golden Pavilion, is a Zen Buddhist Temple in Kyoto. Its official name is Rokuon-ji(鹿苑寺), meaning "Deer Garden Temple". The temple is best known for its top two floors being covered entirely in gold leaf, which reflect exquisitely in the surrounding pond, creating one of Japan's most iconic and peaceful scenery. The gold was used for spirituals purposes, as it was thought to purify pollution or any negative thoughts and any feelings towards death, as well as visual symbolic ones in a period that heavily relied on building aesthetics. Kinkaku-Ji was originally built between 1397-1399 during the Muromachi period. The current structure has been rebuilt in 1955 after it was destroyed in an arson attack.

🟢 4. Tokyo Skytree(東京スカイツリー, Tōkyō Sukaitsurī)

Tokyo Skytree(東京スカイツリー, Tōkyō Sukaitsurī) is a 634-meter-tall television and radio broadcasting site for the Kantō region,as well as being an observation tower, its self being located in Sumida, Tokyo. Its height was intentionally chosen as a reference to "Musashi"(6-3-4), an old for the Tokyo region. It was completed in 2012, and is the third tallest structures in the world. It replaces the older Tokyo tower because it was ineffective due to being surrounded by high rise buildings. The name "sky" and "tree" symbolizes connectivity and growth. Its structure is meticulously designed, starting with a triangular base that gradually becomes a circular shaft, a form that improves stability and air resistance. Its known for its nighttime lighting, featuring two main illuminations that alternate daily, "Iki", a sky blue color representing "stylish" and "Miyabi",a purple color representing elegance and refinement, using LED to achieve this. Its exterior lattice is painted a color officially called "Skytree White", this is an original color based on bluish-white traditional Japanese called aijiro.

🟤 5. Himeji Castle(姫路城)

Himeji Castle (姫路城) is located in Himeji and is considered the finest surviving example of traditional Japanese castles. It is the largest and most visited castle in Japan, featuring a complex network of 83 advanced defensive systems from the feudal period. It is frequently referred to as Hakuro-jō, meaning White Egret Castle or Shirasagi-jō, meaning White Heron Castle. This is due to its brilliant white exterior and its supposed resemblance to a bird taking flight. Its origins date back to 1333, when Akamatsu Norimura built the first fort on top of Himeyama Hill. At the centre of the castle stands the tenshu(天守)(main keep), which is the most prominent structure of Japanese castles. It served as a storehouse in times of peace and as a fortified tower in times of war. Around it were the residences and government offices of the daimyo (大名, feudal lord), surrounded by yagura(櫓)(turrets). Recently it underwent restoration work for several years and reopened to the public on March 27, 2015.
